TAVI/TAVR for Aortic Stenosis: Minimally Invasive Valve Replacement

TAVI/TAVR (Transcatheter Aortic Valve Implantation/Replacement) is a minimally invasive option to treat aortic stenosis (narrowed aortic valve) without open-heart surgery in suitable patients.

No chest opening

Catheter-based approach for selected patients.

Suitable for many elderly/high-risk

Eligibility depends on risk + anatomy.

Shorter stay

Often 2–3 days in hospital (typical; varies).

Symptom relief

Breathlessness/fatigue may improve after recovery.

Advanced imaging guidance

CT + Echo/TEE used for planning.

Team-based decision

Multidisciplinary heart team evaluation.

Signs you may need assessment

  • Valve leakage (backflow) through the mitral valve
  • Chest pain/pressure
  • Dizziness or fainting
  • Ongoing fatigue and reduced stamina

Tests commonly used

  • Echocardiography (Echo) to confirm severity
  • CT scan to plan access and valve sizing
  • TEE/3D imaging when needed for details

MitraClip / MyClip (TEER): Non-Surgical Mitral Valve Repair

TEER (Transcatheter Edge-to-Edge Repair) is a catheter-based option to reduce mitral regurgitation (leaky mitral valve) without open-heart surgery in suitable patients. It is commonly known as MitraClip (and similar devices such as MyClip).

No open-heart incision

Catheter-based approach for selected patients.

Imaging-guided precision

Planned with Echo/TEE assessment.

Often quicker recovery

Many patients mobilise early (varies by health).

Lower surgical stress

Considered for higher surgical-risk patients.

Shorter stay

Often 2–3 days in hospital (typical; varies).

Goal: reduce leakage

Relief in symptoms depends on severity and health.

Who may be considered

  • Severe mitral regurgitation with significant symptoms
  • Patients at higher risk for open-heart surgery (as per evaluation)
  • Valve anatomy suitable on Echo/TEE review

Bring/Upload: Echo report • TEE (if done) • discharge summaries • medicine list

MitraClip vs MyClip

  • Both are TEER devices. The treating team decides what fits best based on valve anatomy and imaging findings.
